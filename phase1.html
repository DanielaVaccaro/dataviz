PHASE 1

Digging into research trends to understand, besides popularity and unpopularity patterns among the set of instruments, the general and broader cultural framework in which people carry out their web researches, has been our first goal. The analysis of search queries trends variations during time made us focus on peaks as singular moments where an external cultural agent or event worked as a trigger for cultural awareness processes.

What emerges from this step is the influence that mass culture and mainstream information consumption patterns have on cultural contents fruition in terms of big numbers. What are people looking for and why are they doing it can be therefore considered the fundamental questions
behind this research phase, supporting the willingness to go beyond simple trend results to foster a meaningful reflection on contemporary patterns of cultural consumption.

INSIGHTS

Comparing the two charts, it is possible to see how Baidu results present on average higher research trends; despite this difference, Guqin, Erhu, Pipa, and Suona turn out to be the most popular instruments in both cases. A qualitative analysis unveiled trigger events that led to remarkable research peaks, on a monthly basis in the case of Google and on a daily basis in the case of Baidu.

Google line chart presents two main disruptive peaks for Guqin and Pipa. The significant rise in the amount of Guqin related researches during summer 2008 turns out to be directly influenced by its appearance in the opening ceremony of the 2008 Beijing Olympic Games, demonstrating the significant impact mass events can exert on online research patterns. On the contrary, the case of Pipa turns out to be a false positive due to the protests against PIPA (PROTECT IP Act) and SOPA (Stop Online Piracy Act).

A direct connection between mass events or mass media culture and online research patterns also emerges from Baidu data, where Erhu reaches the highest peak with a research heat of 23467. The trigger events, in this case, consists of a quiz proposed by Alipay to offer a discount; “how many strings does an Erhu have?”. The massive gap between this data and the average research heat of the instrument expands the cultural discourse, underlining how marketing practices and strategies profoundly can shape cultural consumption.
