? The initial dataset, provided by the Shanghai Conservatory of music and constituted by 115 traditional instruments, has been reduced to facilitate the following research steps.

Firstly, to avoid language ambiguity and conduct the research at an international level, every instrument has been matched with its English name or pinyin (when the English name does not exist). Every instrument's name has been verified through research on Google and Baidu search engines to ascertain the usage of the term.

Secondly, instruments that differentiate from others only in terms of dimensions or ethnicity - and therefore were not recognized outside China - have been grouped under families while those who have no search results on Google have been deleted. The result is a selection of 62 instruments.
