Online cultural
consumption patterns:

An analysis on chinese traditional instruments

← Back to the project

This research moves from a general reflection on the relationship between cultural consumption patterns and traditional music as a part of our cultural heritage. Always more frequent digitalization practices carried out by cultural institutions, but also content production through thematic websites and personal blogs managed by highly interested enthusiasts, conducted to a significant availability of cultural contents online. The internet can be, therefore, considered one of the main spaces for cultural consumption and production, shaping, through its intrinsical medium nature, fruition practices, and cultural consumption patterns.

Aim of the research is a data-driven investigation on the online existence of chinese traditional instruments, both in terms of production and consumption patterns, to trigger a wider societal discourse on the mutual relationship and influence between cultural heritage and the internet as a tool of cultural transformation.

The research takes into account 62 selected musical instruments, coming from an initial dataset, collected and produced by the Department of Music Technology, China Conservatory of Music, proceeding with a parallel and comparative analysis concerning web search queries’ trends and research results on two different search engines: Google, as an international research window and Baidu, as a Chinese one.
